---
title: "A field of Azaleas"
date: "2025-05-11"
category: "poem"
---

How rapid the course of time, how unforgiving.
The permanence of action, the waning moon.

How easy it is to lose oneself in the merciless.
No weight to abstraction, and neither to the living.

How easy it is to resort to violence, to the flame.
We burn what we love, and love what we burn.

How easy it is to lose oneself, but who are we to blame?
We are three sided, we wander blindly, unguided.

Are we too far gone, is it perhaps too late?
We dwell on what could have been, what should have been.

How difficult it is to face oneself, to face our ideas.
We cannot rely on sacrifice, nor borrowed panaceas.

So let go of fear, open the dragon’s cage.
Open your eyes, and find yourself in a field of Azaleas.
