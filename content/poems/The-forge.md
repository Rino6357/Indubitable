---
title: "The forge"
date: "2025-05-14"
category: "poem"
---

Marvels of war lay idle, hung to dry,
A gaping furnace, shrouded, burning.

An anvil, a hammer, and prongs to pry,
A submerged salvation, hidden, yearning.

A reluctant forfeit, a path forked,
Blindly we walk, unaware, gone.

A bottle of wine, aging, corked,
Perhaps a new beginning, yet only a pawn.

A hesitant weapon, yet the forge is all bending,
The furnace glows hotter, growing, inviting.

A searing pain, for without there exists no change,
Constant, a paradox, yet the forge is all mending.

Liquefied, destroyed, and created once again,
Poured into a mold, monstrous, imperfect, we evolve.

We seek the basin, the water, escape, salvation,
The forge knows better, however, with great resolve.

The crash of the hammer, the rigidness of the anvil,
A great inferno, acceleration, a collision.

Our fight gone, submerged, one with the water,
From the ashes reborn, to the forge we are sworn.

Yet the world awaits, our passion burning hotter,
Apart, yet together, for from the forge we were born.
