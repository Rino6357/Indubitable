---
title: "The Iceman"
date: "2026-02-04"
category: "poem"
---

Metal against paper, black versus white, Sweat on my palms, loosening my grip.
The floor was red, unrecognizable.
What had I been wielding?
What had I been brandishing?
Are my hands forever bound to the hilt?
Was this the doom of the world I'd built?
Cold metal against my skin,
Black blood runs down the corpse, A corpse of paper and runes.
The peace and redemption I had sought,
Maybe he, perhaps,
Could succeed where I could not.
The one who shall be reborn, The sharp end of my blade,
An edge clean of that wicked crimson, The chance of peace for which I had prayed.
If he,
The core of all that is left of my soul,
Could find it, Then perhaps, I could attain it,
I could attain my goal.
